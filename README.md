Title: Analyzing Death Rate Disparities by Race, Sex, and Time in New York City

Objective: Analyze disparities in NYC death rates across race, sex, and time to identify long-term public health trends

Import Packages: Pandas, NumPy, and Matplotlib

Source: NYC Open Data

Project Outline Steps:
- Provided an overview of the dataset and analysis objectives
- Defined core analytical questions to guide the project
- Cleaned and transformed raw data for analysis
- Conducted exploratory data analysis (EDA)
- Identified demographic and temporal trends within the dataset
- Created visualizations to examine disparities in leading causes of death across demographic groups, gender differences, and changes over time

Key Findings:
- Heart disease showed the highest mortality disparity rates across all demographic groups within the dataset.
- Non-Hispanic Black populations experienced the highest disparity rates among the top five leading causes of death, with the exception of COVID-19, where mortality rates were more comparable to the Hispanic population.
- Mortality rates among males consistently exceeded those of females across leading causes of death, with disparity gaps in several categories approaching nearly double the rate.
- Heart disease remained the leading cause of mortality disparity consistently from 2007 through 2021, demonstrating persistent long-term trends over time.
